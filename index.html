Melissa Dalgleish

PhD Candidate (ABD), M.A., B.Ed., Hons. B.A.

Hi! I'm a PhD candidate (ABD) in English at York University in Toronto, Ontario. I'm also a Graduate Fellow with Editing Modernism in Canada.My dissertation traces a literary history of three poets--Anne Wilkinson, Jay Macpherson, and James Reaney--at the core of the turn towards mythopoeia among modernist poets in Canada between the late 1940s and early 1960s. In opposition to the current nationally- and textually-focused studies of Canadian modernism, I explore the international flows of aesthetic and affect, and complex social networks, that create new literary communities and new poetic modes. As part of EMiC, I am working on a social-text edition of Anne Wilkinson's 1951 collection of poetry, Counterpoint to Sleep.
